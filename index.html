Personality Tests

Big Five

The Big Five are widely accepted in psychology as measureable personality traits that exist in everyone. These include: openness, agreableness, conscientiousness, negative emotionality, and extraversion. Each trait is scored on a continuum from no identification with the trait, to complete identification.

MBTI

The Myers-Briggs Type Indicator was the brain child of Isabel Briggs Myers and her daughter, Katherine. Both explored Jungian psychology in order to create an accessible personality assessment tool. There are 16 types in all. Each is derived from the dichotimization of four key elements within a bidirectional structure of typical day-to-day behavior.

DISC

The DISC Model was formulated by psychologist, William Marston. The assessment was created by psychologists years later. It is geared toward creating an understanding of workplace behavior and improving leadership. It measures two dimensions of behavior: an individual's perception of the nature of their environment, and self-perception of control within the environment.

Enneagram

The Enneagram of Personality is largely credited to psychiatrist, Claudia Naranjo. Drawing from the foundational work of others, he devised the 9 point enneagram structure that is widely known today. With origins based on spiritualism, this tool emphasizes the function of the ego, basic fear and vice, as well as how to overcome these.

Four Temperaments

The Four Temperaments were formulated by Hippocrates, a physician who lived in early BC. He devised this theory of personality by observing behavior as either hot/cold and wet/dry. The types were also associated with bodily fluids and an excess or absence of one. These fluids were blood, yellow bile, black bile, and phlegm.
