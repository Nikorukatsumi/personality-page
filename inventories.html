Big Five

Background:
A five-factor model of personality was first conceptualized in the 30s as consisting of elements: intellect, character, temperment, disposition, and temper. Since then, it has been revisited and reformed many times over into the model it has become today; these traits being: openness, agreableness, conscientiousness, negative emotionality, and extraversion.

Source: http://laurenteffel.com/19-inspirational-big-5-personality-traits-chart/personality-traits-noba-intended-for-19-inspirational-big-5-personality-traits-chart/

Purpose:
This test is designed to help individuals understand where they fall in the spectrum of these traits. Rather than dictate whether someone is open or not, agreable or not, this tool was meant to help define to what degree a person has these traits - as all people share these in common.

MBTI

Background:
The Myers Briggs Personality Type Indicator measures personality using 4 factors. These include: Extroverted(E) or Introverted(I), Sensing(S) or Intuiting(N), Feeling(F) or Thinking(T), and Judging(J) or Perceiving(P). In total, there are 16 combinations of these traits (e.g., ESFJ or INTP), conceptualizing 16 unique personalities.

Purpose:
This test is commonly used in college settings focused around career placement, as well as the workplace to encourage empathy and teamwork. And, although it has been criticized by researchers as a tool not to be taken too seriously, many find the MBTI to be fun and informative.

Created by Isabel Briggs Myers and Katharine Briggs

DISC

Background:
The DISC assessment was created from the works of William Marston who studied human emotion and behavior. He found that emotions impacted behavior significantly and variably across the lifespan. In the 40s, William Clarke created an assessment tool for employers, noting that his measures of personality were similar to Marston's early work. Since then, the DISC has undergone many revisions and continues to be useful to employers and individuals today.

Purpose:
The DISC test posits that personality and behavior is influenced by an individual's perception of the favorability of their environment, as well as their perception of control/lack of control.It relies of four factors: dominance(D), influence(I), steadiness(S), and conscientiousness(C).

Source: https://united-partners.com/how-disc-personality-assessment-can-make-our-life-at-work-better/

Created by Walter Clarke

Enneagram

Background:
The Enneagram was formulated by Oscar Ichazo, a Bolivian philosopher. He championed the enneagram as part of an earlier work called Protoanalysis. In the Enneagram, an emphasis is placed on the ego and it's impact on thoughts and behaviors. By identifying a type, one can better identify their ego fixations, and seek to remedy them.

Purpose:
The Enneagram comprises nine principle personalities in which one will be most relevant to the individual. From each number, two arrows point toward one number, representing and individuals personality when under stress, and another number, representing behavior when doing well. Each number will also score a subtype, which is called a wing.

Source: http://infographicfacts.com/9-body-types/the-9-body-types/

Created by Oscar Ichazo
